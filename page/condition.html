    Common Causes of Lameness in Pets

    • Sprains and strains: These are often the result of overexertion or minor accidents.
    • Arthritis: A degenerative condition that affects joints, especially in older pets.
    • Fractures or dislocations: Trauma can lead to more severe injuries requiring prompt attention.
    • Infections or abscesses: Puncture wounds or infections can cause pain and limping.
    • Nerve damage: Spinal or neurological issues can affect a petâ€™s ability to move properly.

    If your pet shows signs of lameness or limping, scheduling a wellness exam at our animal hospital can help pinpoint the issue and start an appropriate treatment plan.

    Signs and Symptoms to Watch For

    • Reluctance to walk, run, or jump
    • Swelling in limbs or joints
    • Visible wounds or abrasions
    • Vocalizing pain when touched or moved
    • Changes in posture or gait

    If you notice any of these signs, consult with a veterinarian near you for a thorough assessment.

    • Physical therapy: To aid recovery and improve mobility.
    • Surgery: Necessary for severe injuries, such as fractures or ligament tears.
    • Weight management: Helps reduce stress on joints and improve overall mobility.
