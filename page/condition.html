Common Causes of Lameness in Pets

Lameness can result from a variety of factors, including injuries, infections, and chronic conditions. Some common causes include:

  • •

    Sprains and strains: These are often the result of overexertion or minor accidents.

  • •

    Arthritis: A degenerative condition that affects joints, especially in older pets.

  • •

    Fractures or dislocations: Trauma can lead to more severe injuries requiring prompt attention.

  • •

    Infections or abscesses: Puncture wounds or infections can cause pain and limping.

  • •

    Nerve damage: Spinal or neurological issues can affect a pet's ability to move properly.

If your pet shows signs of lameness or limping, scheduling a wellness exam at our animal hospital can help pinpoint the issue and start an appropriate treatment plan.
