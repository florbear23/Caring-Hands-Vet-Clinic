    PET EMERGENCY

    What to Do After a Pet Fight: A Guide from Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic

    Sep. 29, 2022

    Unfortunately, pet owners may experience a pet emergency at one point in life. Pet emergencies include fights, sudden illnesses, poisoning, or animal attacks. These incidences can be scary and may confuse you. For example, what happens when your pet gets involved in a fight? There are several things you should do as a pet owner. We at Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic in Philadelphia, PA, are here to help your pet after a pet fight. Come visit us to get help from a vet on our team.

    Keep Calm

    Your first reaction obviously would be panic, but you must stay calm and assess the scene. Try not to yell, and don't be physical to your pets. They might pose a threat to you also. If they are still fighting, find a way to break up the fight without endangering yourself.

    Keep Your Pet Still

    After a pet fight, keeping your pet as still and quiet as possible is best. Make sure the movement is minimal, especially if you suspect an injury or a broken bone.

    Call Our Animal Hospital

    Call our animal clinic and let them know what to expect ahead of time. Seek vet care services if your pet is injured. Even though your pet may appear to only have small cuts or bruises, the wounds could get infected.

    Safely Transport Your Pet

    When your pet has an emergency, ask for assistance to move your pet. During your call to our animal hospital, you can ask how to move your injured pet safely. It can help prevent biting or inadvertent scratching from your injured pet.

    Know the Proper Way of Handling Your Injured Pet

    An injured pet will most likely be in pain, disoriented, and panicked. Pet emergency stress can cause a calm and friendly pet to become aggressive. Your pet might respond in different ways. Nevertheless, it's essential to be cautious when handling an injured pet.

    Visit Us for Pet Care from a Veterinarian on Our Veterinary Team

    Has your pet been involved in a fight? Feel free to contact us at Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic in Philadelphia, PA. Our team of professionals can provide your pet with pet care services for a pet injured after a fight. Call us at (215) 621-8959 for help from a veterinarian on our veterinary team.
