    Treating Fall Pet Allergies in Philadelphia: Pet Care Tips

    Sep. 15, 2021

    Just like humans, pets can suffer from the overwhelming effects of seasonal allergies. Fall pet allergies can cause a range of symptoms, including watery eyes and skin irritation. If you notice your pet scratching or licking themselves excessively, sneezing repeatedly, or experiencing vomiting, swollen skin, or diarrhea, they may be suffering from seasonal allergies. Along with taking your pet to us at Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic for quality pet care, here are some pet care tips to help you treat fall pet allergies.

    Change Walking Times and Route

    The pollen levels are typically the highest during the late afternoon and early morning, so if possible, consider avoiding walking your dog during these times. If walking routes typically include passing parks or fields where there are a lot of flowers, trees, and plants, consider changing your route to avoid these areas.

    Remove the Allergens

    When returning home from walks, make sure to use a wipe to remove allergens from your petâ€™s skin and fur. Pay close attention to their paws as this is the area of their body that tends to collect the most allergens. These allergens can easily be transferred to the eyes, nose, and mouth when pets touch these areas with their paws.

    Bedding and Toys

    Allergens can build up on your petâ€™s bedding and plush toys, so make sure to clean these items by washing them in hot water at least once a week. Avoid using fabric softener or laundry soap with fragrances as these may increase the risk of skin irritation and cause watery eyes. Be sure to talk with our veterinarian if you have questions about the best bedding options for your pet.

    Diet Supplements

    Dietary supplements can often help to relieve symptoms of fall pet allergies. For instance, our vet may recommend dietary supplements such as omega-6 or omega-3 oil to help reduce itchiness and improve your petâ€™s skin health. Remember to make sure your petâ€™s water bowls are clean, contain freshwater, and are free from any risk of contaminants.

    Contact Our Animal Hospital for Quality Pet Care from Our Philadelphia Vet

    If your pet continues to struggle with symptoms of allergies, make a veterinary appointment at our animal hospital. Depending on the cause and severity of the symptoms, our vet may prescribe treatments like antihistamines or allergy shots.
