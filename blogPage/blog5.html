    After-Care Tips Following Your Pet’s Spay or Neuter Surgery

    Jul. 29, 2022

    While it can be nerve-wracking to have your pet spayed or neutered, it's the best choice for its health. Spaying and neutering can reduce the risk of serious health problems and improve your pet's quality of life. At Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic in Philadelphia, PA, we're happy to answer your questions about after-care following your pet's surgery.

    Pet Care Tips

    It can take time for an animal to fully recover from spaying and neutering. Most pets will take around two weeks to fully heal. Your pet may also need supervision for a few days after the procedure. Check your pet's incision area each day to make sure that it's healing properly. It's normal for there to be some redness around the site of the incision, but if you see swelling or other worrying signs, reach out to our veterinarian right away.

    You'll need to avoid bathing your pet for at least two weeks while the incision heals. If your pet needs to be cleaned at any point during this time, try using a damp cloth or a dry shampoo instead! Our veterinarian can give you more tips on keeping your pet clean.

    A spay or neuter procedure can be stressful for a pet. This stress can cause pets to behave in unusual ways. It can be helpful to give your pet a quiet place to relax, especially if you have other animals in the house. Play is great for pets, but your pet shouldn't be too active while it's healing. Try to keep your pet from running, jumping, or rolling during its recovery. Don't hesitate to reach out to your vet for more pet care tips!
