    Symptoms of Fall Pet Allergies

    Recognizing the symptoms of pet allergies is the first step to getting your pet the help it needs. Watch for the following signs of allergies:

    • Skin irritation
    • Watery eyes
    • Abnormal scratching
    • Abnormal licking
    • Chewing, biting, or pulling of the skin
    • Chewing, biting, or pulling on the pads of the feet or toes
    • Ear infections
    • Diarrhea
    • Reverse sneezing
    • Watery eyes
    • Hair loss
    • Sores
    • Redness of the skin
    • Skin infections
    • Scratching and shaking the head and ears

    If your pet seems to have an itch that it cannot leave alone, it is time to contact our veterinarian.

    Common Causes of Pet Allergies

    While allergies are the most noticeable in the spring months, they are still prevalent in the fall. Many plants do not pollinate until the fall, which causes most fall allergies in humans and animals. A few of the fall pollinating plants include:

    • Cockleburr
    • Curly dock
    • Goldenrod
    • Lamb‚Äôs Quarters
    • Pigweed
    • Plaintain
    • Ragweed
    • Russian thistle
    • Sagebrush

    If your pet has long-lasting allergies that do not go away after winter is well established, your pet may be allergic to other environmental factors. These other factors could be mold, dust mites, plant allergies, flea allergies, indoor surface allergies (carpeting or furniture), or laundry detergent allergies. If you live in an area that does not freeze, pollen and allergens will continue to build up, making life increasingly more uncomfortable for your pet.

    Treatment for Fall Allergies

    You will need to have a veterinarian examine your pet to determine the culprit of the allergies and prescribe an appropriate treatment. Once diagnosed, we may give your pet medication such as steroids or antihistamines, a medicated shampoo, or prescribe desensitization therapy.
