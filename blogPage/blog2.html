    Preparing Your Pet for Pet Surgery from a Veterinarian Near You in Philadelphia, PA

    Nov. 06, 2024

    Taking your pet into an animal hospital or veterinarian's office for pet surgery can feel daunting and extremely stressful. Thankfully, at the Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic, your veterinarian in Philadelphia, PA, we can walk you through the process of preparing your pet for any upcoming procedure so everything can go as smoothly as possible.

    Conduct a Pre-Surgical Checkup

    Take your pet to the vet or animal hospital in advance before their scheduled surgery. Your pet may undergo a physical examination, traditional blood work, and diagnostic imaging.

    Fasting Instructions

    Anytime your pet is undergoing a surgical procedure that requires anesthesia, your vet near you will provide guidance in terms of fasting. For dogs and cats, it's typically recommended that they fast 8 to 12 hours before an upcoming procedure to minimize risks and to ensure the procedure goes as smoothly as possible.

    Medication

    You'll also want to consider what type of medication your pet is already on when scheduling an upcoming pet surgery. Some medications may need to be paused before an upcoming surgery, whereas others are fine to take before and after a procedure. Speak directly to a trusted pet surgery veterinarian at Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic to maintain your peace of mind.

    Preparing for Aftercare

    Knowing what to expect after your pet's surgery is done will also help you to keep your own mind at ease. Speak to your preferred veterinarian at Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic about anxiety medications, natural supplements, and other calming supplement solutions that can help your pet readjust at home once the procedure is complete. The more comfortable they feel returning home, the less likely they will be to keep exhibiting stress-related signs and symptoms.

    Get Pet Surgery and Animal Hospital Services from a Veterinarian Near You

    Ensuring your pet is as ready as possible before surgery at a local vet or animal hospital can be important for the best outcome possible. Whether you're interested in neutering your pet or if they require a more extensive surgical procedure, we at Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic in Philadelphia, PA, are here for you.
