    PET NUTRITION

    Frequently Asked Questions About Pet Nutrition Answered by a Veterinarian in Philadelphia, PA

    Jan. 27, 2022

    As a pet owner, you want to do everything you can to ensure your furry friend is as healthy as possible. But with all the conflicting information out there on pet nutrition, it can be hard to know where to start. We're going to look at some frequently asked questions about pet nutrition and provide you with crucial information you need to make informed decisions about your pet's diet.

    If you need pet nutritional advice in Philadelphia, PA, you can always visit our veterinarian at Caring Hands Veterinary Clinic. We're here to answer any of your questions and address any concerns you may have about feeding your precious animal.

    Why is pet nutrition important?

    Every animal needs nutritious food to survive. Nutrition not only provides animals with the energy they need to live, but it also helps them grow and maintain their health. A proper diet keeps animals' immune systems strong, their coats shiny, and their bodies fit. Poor nutrition, on the other hand, can lead to a number of health problems and potentially shorten an animal's lifespan.

    What should I feed my pet?

    The most important part of a good diet is that you're feeding your animal quality pet food. You wouldn't poor quality food, and your pet shouldn't either. Be sure to read the label before buying any pet food. If the bag or can doesn't provide a complete list of ingredients, chances are good that the food is full of fillers, preservatives, and other chemicals your animal doesn't need.

    How much food should I feed my pet?

    That depends on several factors, including your pet's age, weight, and energy level. A veterinarian can help you determine the right amount of food for your pet. Feeding your furry friend too much food can lead to obesity and other serious health problems. Feeding your animal too little can result in malnutrition and starvation.

    What are the main ingredients to look for in pet food?

    The top three ingredients in good pet food are animal-based protein, healthy fats, and fiber. You want to see a lot of protein and fiber, with not too much fat. And look for whole ingredients over artificial flavors and colors. Other vital nutrients are calcium, phosphorus, iron, and zinc, and these are crucial for better bone growth.

    Do I need to change my pet's diet as he or she gets older?

    Yes. As your pet ages, his nutritional needs change. Puppies and kittens have very high nutritional needs, as their bodies are still growing. They need a lot of protein to power all that growth. Adult dogs and cats, on the other hand, have different nutritional needs. You'll need to read the label on pet food carefully and choose a product that provides the right amount of nutrients for your furry friend's age and lifestyle.
